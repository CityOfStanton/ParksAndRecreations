# Rule 1.10 Bat Rules

The bat must be a baseball bat which meets the USA Baseball Bat standard (USABat) as adopted by Little League. It shall be a smooth, rounded stick, and made of wood or of material and color tested and proved acceptable to the USA Baseball Bat standard (USABat).

- **No USSSA bats allowed.**
- **Umpires will check bats before each game.** Bats must be lined up on the outside fence.  
  - If an illegal bat is used during game play, the player will be suspended for the remainder of the game and must sit out the next game.
  - If the next scheduled game is rained out and not played, the player will sit out the next playable game.

# Rule 3.0 Game Preliminaries

### (a)
Require strict observance of all rules governing team personnel and implements of play, and confirm with the managers of both teams at the plate conference that all equipment is proper and legal for play according to Little League rules.

**NOTE:**  
Umpires are not required to inspect equipment prior to the start of the game. The manager of each team is responsible for ensuring that all equipment is legal and proper for play according to Little League rules.

**PENALTY:**  
If illegal equipment is used during the game:
- The team manager will be ejected.
- The player who used the illegal equipment will be ejected.
- Both the manager and player will be suspended for their team’s next physically played game and may not be in attendance at the game site, including pregame and postgame activities.

### (b)
Ensure that all playing lines (heavy lines on Diagrams No. 1 and No. 2) are marked with non-caustic lime, chalk, or other white material easily distinguishable from the ground or grass.

---

## Rule 3.03 Re-entry

A player in the starting lineup who has been removed for a substitute may re-enter the game in the **same position in the batting order**, provided:

### (a)
The substitute has completed one time at bat. For Mandatory Play purposes (traditional batting order), a time at bat is completed when one of the following occurs:
- The player is retired as a batter
- The player is retired as a batter-runner
- The player reaches base and scores
- After reaching base, the inning or game ends

**A.R.:**  
If, while at bat, the third out of the half-inning is recorded by retiring another runner before any of the above occurs, the batter must return as the first batter in the next half-inning.

**NOTE (Mandatory Play – Regular Season Only):**  
When appearing offensively for the first time, a player must remain in the game until one of the following occurs:
- Retired as a batter
- Retired as a batter-runner
- Reaches base and scores
- After reaching base, the inning or game ends

### (b)
Has played defensively for a minimum of six (6) consecutive outs.

### (c)
Pitchers once removed from the mound may not return as pitchers.

# Rule 4.0 Start and End of Game

## Rule 4.01
The umpires shall proceed directly to home plate just before the scheduled game time and meet with both managers.

Sequence:
1. Home team manager provides the batting order (duplicate copies) to the Umpire-in-Chief.
2. Visiting manager provides the batting order (duplicate copies) to the Umpire-in-Chief.
3. The Umpire-in-Chief verifies both copies and distributes one to each opposing manager.  
   - The original retained by the umpire is the official batting order.
4. Once the home team’s batting order is submitted, the umpires assume authority over the field and determine when a game is called, halted, or resumed due to weather or field conditions.

---

# Rule 5.0 Live Ball

## Rule 5.01
At game time, the Umpire-in-Chief orders the home team to take defensive positions and the first visiting batter to the batter’s box. When all players are in position, the umpire calls **“Play.”**

## Rule 5.08
If a thrown ball accidentally touches a base coach, or a pitched/thrown ball touches an umpire, the ball remains alive.  
- If the base coach interferes with a thrown ball, the runner is out.

## Rule 5.09 Dead Ball Situations

The ball becomes dead and runners advance or return without liability when:

- **(a)** A pitched ball touches a batter in a legal batting position (forced runners advance).
- **(b)** Plate umpire interferes with the catcher’s throw (runners return unless the out is recorded).
- **(c)** An illegal pitch is committed.
- **(d)** A ball is illegally batted.
- **(e)** A foul ball is not caught.
- **(f)** A fair ball touches a runner or umpire before passing an infielder (runner is out).

**NOTE:**  
If the ball passes or is deflected by an infielder and then touches a runner, the ball remains live.

- **(g)** A pitched ball lodges in the catcher’s or umpire’s equipment.
  - On third strike or fourth ball, batter is awarded first base and runners advance.
- **(h)** Intermediate/Junior/Senior: A legal pitch touches a runner attempting to score.

## Rule 5.10 Calling “Time”

The Umpire-in-Chief shall call “Time” when:
- Weather or darkness prevents play
- Light failure occurs
- An accident incapacitates a player or umpire
- A manager requests time for substitution or conference  
  - Only one offensive time-out per inning
- The umpire examines the ball or consults a manager
- A fielder falls into a dead-ball area after a catch
- A player or person is removed from the field

**NOTE:**  
No umpire may call “Time” while a play is in progress except as noted.

## Rule 5.11
Play resumes when the pitcher is on the mound with the ball and the plate umpire calls **“Play.”**

# Rule 6.0 Batter

## Rule 6.01
- Batters must hit in the listed order.
- The next inning’s leadoff batter follows the last batter who legally completed an at-bat.

**NOTE:**  
If the third out is made on a runner while a batter is at bat, that batter leads off the next inning with a fresh count.

## Rule 6.02 Batter’s Box Conduct
- Batter must enter promptly.
- Batter may not leave after the pitcher starts the motion.

**Local League Option:**  
Batter must keep at least one foot in the box during the at-bat, with listed exceptions.

**PENALTY:**  
After one warning, a strike is called.  
- Minor/Major: Ball is dead.  
- Intermediate/Junior/Senior: Ball remains live.

## Rule 6.03
Both feet must be within the batter’s box.

## Rule 6.04
An at-bat is complete when the batter is retired or becomes a runner.

## Rule 6.05 Batter Is Out When:
- A fly ball is legally caught
- Bunting foul on third strike
- Hit by own batted ball
- Fair ball hits batter before a fielder
- Bat hits ball a second time in fair territory
- Intentionally deflecting a foul ball
- Tagged before reaching first base
- Infielder intentionally drops a ball
- Interference by a preceding runner

---

# Rule 7.0 The Runner

## Rule 7.01
A runner earns the right to a base by touching it before being retired and retains it until retired or forced.

## Rule 7.03
Two runners may not occupy a base.
- If both are tagged, the trailing runner is out.
- If forced, the lead runner is out.

---

## Additional Notes
- If a runner is hit by a ball before it reaches a fielder, the runner is out.
- **9/10:** Ball is dead when returned to the pitcher on the mound.
- **7/8:** Ball is dead when returned to the pitching circle (must be thrown to the player acting as pitcher). Advancement and return lines will be used.
